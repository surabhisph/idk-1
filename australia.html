Australia

Australia is a continent as well as a country and it's known for its kangaroos and koalas. It surrounds itself with the Indian and Pacific Ocean and New Zealand, Indonesia, New Calidonia, Papau New Guinea, Vanuatu, Soloman Islands, andTimor-Leste. It's not only known for it's wildlife, but also it's beaches. About 85% of the population of this archipelago live within 50 kilo-meters of the coast. Some of it's popular cities include, Sydney, Melbourn, Brisbane, and ofcourse, its capital, Canberra.

Sydney

Sydney! Sydney is one of the most popular cities in Australia and the world. It is known for it's Sydney Opera House. Did you know Sydney is the most popular metropolitan area in Australia. You can go to the famous Sydney Opera House, The Sydney Harbour Bridge, you can walk along the Bodi Beach, and you can learn about Australia by going to the Darling Harbour. The Syndney Opera House, is know because it has lots of performing venues. They are:
    1) Concert Hall: With 2,679 seats, the home of the Sydney Symphony Orchestra. It contains the Sydney Opera House Grand Organ, the largest mechanical tracker action organ in the world, with over 10,000 pipes.
    2) Joan Sutherland Theatre: A theatre with 1,507 seats, the Sydney home of Opera Australia and The Australian Ballet.
    3) Drama Theatre: A theatre with 544 seats, used by the Sydney Theatre Company.
    4) Studio: A studio with 280 permanent seats and a maximum capacity of 400.
    5) Utzon Room: A small multi-purpose venue for parties, corporate functions and small productions
    6) A Recording Studio
    7) Outdoor Forecourt: An open-air venue

The Great Barrier Reef

The Great Barrier Reef is not only Australia's largest coral reef system, but also the worlds. It streches over 2,300 kilometers which is about 1,400 miles. It is world's biggest single structure made by living organisms. This reef is made out of coral polyps. Although the reef is collapsing due to plastics, a very big part of the GBR is being protected by the Great Barrier Reef Marine Park. The GBR is facing a lot of threats along with golbal warming, water pollution, and pesticides. This can be stopped. The way that we can stop this is by stop polluting the oceans, stop using plastic, and stop polluting the air because that is what causes global warming.

The Great Barrier is home to more than 1,500 fish species. For example, the clownfish, red bass, red-throat emperor, and several species of snapper and coral trout. Forty-nine species mass spawn, while eighty-four other species spawn elsewhere in their range. Seventeen species of sea snake live on the Great Barrier Reef in warm waters up to 50 metres deep and are more common in the southern than in the northern section. None found in the Great Barrier Reef World Heritage Area are endemic, nor are any endangered.
