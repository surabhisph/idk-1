The pyramids are huge tombs for pharaohs and nobility. Pharaohs are kings or queens. The Egyptians believed that dead people’s spirits could live after death if their bodies were carefully preserved. It was especially important to preserve a pharaoh’s bodies because their spirit would help Egypt’s kingdom to survive. The slaves make dead bodies into mummies and, buried them in these excellent tombs along with spices, clothes, jewels and things they need for afterlife. The first pyramid was built between 2630 and 2611 BC. It was built for King Djoser. Before that, pharaohs (kings or queens) were buried in flat-mounds called mastabas. The last pyramid in Egypt was built around 1530 BC. Thousands of egyptians worked in the hot sun to clear the site, lay the foundations, drag building stone from the quarry and lift it in place. Most of the egyptians were ordinary farmers,who worked as builders to pay their duty to the pharaohs. Expert craftsmen cut the stones into blocks and fitted them together to make a pyramid. When the pyramid was almost finished casing blocks of white limestone were laid on top of the main pyramid blocks. Each casing block was carefully trimmed so that the outers surface of the pyramid would be smooth and white. The casing blocks from the Great Pyramid of Giza were removed in the 14th and 15th centuries and were used to build the city of Cairo(Egypt's capital). Some casing blocks still remain on the top of the pyramid next to Khufu's pyramid(belonging to Khafra's pyramid.

The Nile River: Egyptian civilizations was poor, but thanks to the Nile River, which flooded every year bringing rich, black silt to the farmer’s crops and fields. The desert beyond this strip of farmland was called the “Red Land.” The Nile river was Egypt’s main highway. Boats were used for transporting everything from grain and cattle to blocks of granite taken from quarries. Ceremonial ships made out of cedar wood were used transport state officials and the pharaohs coffin. In 1954 a boat made for King Khufu was discovered in a pit next to the Great Pyramid. Built around 4,500 years ago the ceremonial barge was 142 feet(43) long and was probably intended for King Khufu’s journey with the Sun-god which is called Ra in afterlife. Farmers dug channel (tunnels) to Egypts great cities lay on the Nile river, which was also used for carrying people, goods, and foods. Lots of Egyptians used to ride boats and, still do. Egyptians ride boats for many things. Sometimes for food, trade, and other stuff,or maybe just for fun. The Nile can be very helpful in Egypt’s dry desert. In Egypt, some Egyptians used fishing nets to catch fish in the Nile river. The Egyptians lived on the a strip on each side of the Nile. Early boats were made out of papyrus. Papyrus are reeds. Later boats and ships were made out of wood. Thorlteyerdhal sailed his papyrus ship"RA" from Egypt to USA(United States of America), showing that a papyrus at could survive an ocean crossing. Skiffs are made out of bundles of papyrus reeds tied together with twine. They are propelled with two(2) oarsmen and are linked by a net edged with floats. Papyrus can also be made into sandals and paper. Egyptians were one of the first people to invent paper.
